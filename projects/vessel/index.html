Lightweight Method for Interactive 3D Medical Image Segmentation with Multi-Round Result Fusion

Bingzhi Shen1, Lufan Chang2,†,‡, Siqi Chen3, Shuxiang Guo1,4,*,†, Hao Liu2
1Beijing Institute of Technology 2Yizhun Medical AI Co., Ltd. 3Tsinghua University 4Southern University of Science and Technology
‡Project Lead †Corresponding Author *Fellow, IEEE
arXiv Paper

Demonstration of the Interactive image segmentation.

The overall architecture of our proposed Lightweight Interactive Network (LIM-Net). It consists of a 2D Interaction Module to generate an initial mask from user clicks, a Memory Model to propagate the mask through the entire 3D sequence, and a Multi-Round Result Fusion (MRF) module to select and refine the best masks across multiple interaction rounds for consistent, high-quality segmentation.

Abstract

In medical imaging, precise annotation of lesions or organs is often required. However, 3D volumetric images typically consist of hundreds or thousands of slices, making the annotation process extremely time-consuming and laborious. Recently, the Segment Anything Model (SAM) has drawn widespread attention due to its remarkable zero-shot generalization capabilities in interactive segmentation. While researchers have explored adapting SAM for medical applications, a key question remains: CAN TRADITIONAL CNN NETWORKS ACHIEVE THE SAME STRONG ZERO-SHOT GENERALIZATION IN THIS TASK? In this paper, we propose the Lightweight Interactive Network for 3D Medical Image Segmentation (LIM-Net), a novel approach demonstrating the potential of compact CNN-based models. Built upon a 2D CNN backbone, LIM-Net initiates segmentation by generating a 2D prompt mask from user hints. This mask is then propagated through the 3D sequence via the Memory Module. To refine and stabilize results during interaction, the Multi-Round Result Fusion (MRF) Module selects and merges optimal masks from multiple rounds. Our extensive experiments show LIM-Net's competitive performance, exhibiting stronger generalization to unseen data compared to SAM-based models with competitive accuracy while requiring fewer interactions. Notably, LIM-Net's lightweight design offers significant advantages in deployment and inference efficiency, with low GPU memory consumption suitable for resource-constrained environments.

Table I presents the results from various methods after six rounds of interaction on four datasets. In this study, the rationale for presenting the results of the sixth round in the table is to maintain consistency with the Mem3D method and to assess the performance level that the model can achieve with fewer interactions.

Qualitative comparison on the unseen BTCV dataset.

We evaluated our model's generalization ability on the unseen BTCV dataset. The results show that LIM-Net achieves strong performance on most segmentation tasks despite not being trained on this data. Compared to SAM-B and SAM-Med3D, our method shows significant improvements for several organs, such as the right kidney, aorta, and esophagus, demonstrating commendable generalization ability with only 6 prompt points per volume.

Generalization performance on the EndoVis 18 surgical video test set.

To further test generalization, we applied our model to the EndoVis 18 surgical video dataset without any fine-tuning. Our approach achieves results comparable to, or even surpassing, methods specifically designed and trained for surgical scene segmentation. This highlights the strong generalization capability of our methodology, enabling the effective transfer of features learned from medical imaging domains to the new surgical video domain.

Effectiveness of the Multi-Round Result Fusion (MRF) Module.

The MRF module is designed to stabilize and improve segmentation quality. As shown, without the MRF module (blue line), the Dice score can fluctuate unpredictably between interaction rounds. By contrast, using the MRF module (red line) leads to a consistently increasing trend in segmentation accuracy. This demonstrates the module's effectiveness in selecting the best masks from multiple rounds, ensuring monotonic improvement and enhancing the reliability of the final output.
